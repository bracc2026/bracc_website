EA Program

The Enrolled Agent (EA) qualification is one of the highest professional credentials in US Taxation issued directly by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS), USA.

An EA is a federally authorized US tax professional who can represent taxpayers before the IRS for tax matters, audits, notices, appeals, and compliance-related services.

Over the last decade, EA has become one of the fastest-growing international career opportunities for commerce, accounting, taxation, and finance professionals in India due to the rapid growth of US outsourcing and global accounting firms.

EA Exam Structure


The EA exam is officially called the Special Enrollment Examination (SEE) and consists of 3 parts:

📘 Part 1 — Individuals

Covers US individual taxation concepts including filing status, income, deductions, credits, capital gains, retirement income, tax calculations, and ethics.

🏢 Part 2 — Businesses

Covers taxation of corporations, partnerships, LLCs, S corporations, business deductions, depreciation, payroll taxes, and business compliance.

⚖️ Part 3 — Representation & Procedures

Covers IRS procedures, taxpayer representation, ethics, audits, penalties, collections, appeals, and professional responsibilities.



What It Takes to Pass EA


Passing EA requires consistency, conceptual understanding, and practice with MCQs and taxation scenarios.

Most working professionals in India generally take:

✅ 6 to 12 months to complete all 3 parts
✅ 2 to 4 months preparation per part
✅ Regular revision and mock test practice

The exam is manageable even for beginners if studied with proper guidance and structured preparation.

Eligibility & Registration


There is no minimum degree requirement to appear for EA.

To register for the exam, candidates generally need:

✅ Valid Passport
✅ Government-issued identification
✅ IRS PTIN (Preparer Tax Identification Number)

The PTIN can be applied online through the IRS website.

Brief History of EA


The Enrolled Agent designation dates back to 1884 in the United States.

It was created after the Civil War when professionals were authorized to represent citizens before the US Treasury regarding financial claims and taxation matters.

Today, EA remains one of the most respected US taxation credentials globally and is directly governed by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS).
